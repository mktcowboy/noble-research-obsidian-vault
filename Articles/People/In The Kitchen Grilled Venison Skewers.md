# In The Kitchen: Grilled Venison Skewers

> Browse: [[Maps/Topics/People Articles|Topic: People]] | [[Maps/Sections/People - Recipes and Cooking|Section: Recipes and Cooking]]
> Original: [Noble.org](https://www.noble.org/legacy/2023-fall/in-the-kitchen-grilled-venison-skewers/)
> Concepts: [[Concepts/Wildlife|Wildlife]], [[Concepts/Regenerative Agriculture|Regenerative Agriculture]]
> Keywords: [[Concepts/Regenerative Agriculture|regenerative agriculture]], [[Concepts/Wildlife|wildlife]]

Try this tasty and colorful way to serve up your deer harvest or the gift of venison from a generous friend.

## Ingredients and Supplies:

- ½ cup soy sauce

- ½ cup olive oil

- 1-3 teaspoons minced ginger

- 2 tablespoons lemon juice (1 whole lemon)

- 1 tablespoon honey

- 2-4 garlic cloves, minced

- 4 green onions, chopped

- 1-3 jalapeños, chopped (optional)

- 1-2 pounds venison meat, cut into about 2-inch cubes (or duck, dove or other wild game if preferred)

- Red bell peppers – diced in 1- to 2-inch pieces

- Onions – diced in 1- to 2-inch pieces

- Cherry tomatoes, left whole

- Zucchini – cut in half moons ½-inch thick

- Wooden or metal skewers

- Salt and pepper (optional)

## Instructions:

1. Mix the soy sauce, oil, ginger, lemon juice, honey, garlic, green onions and jalapeños in a bowl.

1. Add the cubed venison and stir to coat.

1. Marinate in the refrigerator for 4 hours or longer.

1. Drain and discard the marinade.

1. Assemble skewers by alternating the venison and vegetables.

1. Grill over medium-to-high heat; stopping at medium is best so venison doesn’t dry out.

1. Serve with your favorite side dish.

5 hours (including marinade) 4-6 serving
